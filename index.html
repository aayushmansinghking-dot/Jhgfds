TAJ MAHAL

The Jewel of Muslim Art in India

Overview

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Architectural Style

The Taj Mahal combines elements from Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles. The most famous feature is the white dome that surmounts the tomb.

Construction

It took approximately 22 years and 20,000 artisans to complete. The construction cost at the time was estimated to be around 32 million rupees.

Visual Splendor

Did You Know?

  • ✨ It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • ✨ The colors of the Taj Mahal change depending on the time of day.
  • ✨ The four minarets are tilted slightly outwards to protect the tomb in case of an earthquake.
