The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the greatest architectural masterpieces in the world, representing love, devotion, and the artistic excellence of the Mughal Empire.

Location

Built By

Emperor Shah Jahan

In Memory Of

Completed

1653 AD

Introduction

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum built on the banks of the Yamuna River. It was constructed by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The monument stands as a symbol of eternal love and unmatched architectural beauty.

History & Biography

Mumtaz Mahal died in 1631 during childbirth, deeply affecting Shah Jahan. To honor her memory, he commissioned the construction of the Taj Mahal. Thousands of artisans, architects, and laborers worked for more than 20 years to complete this masterpiece. The Taj Mahal reflects a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.

Architectural Details

Material Used

White marble from Makrana, Rajasthan

Main Dome

Large central dome surrounded by four smaller domes

Minarets

Four tall minarets symbolizing Islamic architecture

Inlay Work

Precious stones like jade, lapis lazuli, and turquoise

Global Importance

The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It attracts millions of tourists every year and represents India's rich cultural and historical heritage on the global stage.
