The Grand Narrative

Located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India, the Taj Mahal is a breathtaking white marble mausoleum. Built between 1632 and 1653, it stands as the ultimate tribute to love, commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is not just a building; it is a masterpiece of symmetry, engineering, and emotion.

Architectural Visuals

The Fine Details

Architecture

It blends Indo-Islamic and Persian styles. The main dome reaches a height of 73 meters (240 feet), surrounded by four smaller domes and four minarets. Every element is perfectly symmetrical.

The Stones

Built from translucent white marble brought from Makrana, Rajasthan. It is inlaid with 28 types of precious and semi-precious stones, including Lapis Lazuli, Jade, and Crystal.

The Calligraphy

The walls are adorned with verses from the Quran, created by the calligrapher Amanat Khan. The script grows larger as it moves up the wall to appear uniform from the ground.

History & Legend

Over 20,000 workers including stonecutters, painters, and embroidery artists were recruited from across the empire, Central Asia, and Iran. Legend says Shah Jahan planned to build a "Black Taj Mahal" across the river for himself, though he was eventually buried alongside Mumtaz in the original white monument after being imprisoned by his son, Aurangzeb.
