Introduction

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it symbolizes eternal love and devotion. The Taj Mahal is admired for its perfect symmetry, artistic beauty, and historical value.

Biography & History

Mumtaz Mahal died in 1631 while giving birth to their fourteenth child. Grief-stricken, Shah Jahan decided to build a grand monument to honor her. Construction began in 1632 and took more than 20 years. Thousands of artisans, architects, and workers contributed to this masterpiece.

Architectural Details

The Taj Mahal is built using white marble from Makrana, Rajasthan. It combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The central dome, four minarets, intricate carvings, and precious stone inlay work make it one of the finest architectural achievements in history.

Global Importance

The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It attracts millions of tourists every year and represents India’s rich cultural heritage and historical excellence on a global scale.

Gallery

The Taj Mahal appears different at various times of the day — glowing softly at sunrise, shining white under sunlight, and appearing golden under moonlight.
